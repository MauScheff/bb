## What is not working

- Fresh lock-screen wake on Avery is still not reliable.
- The current blocker is not backend config, not Worker signing, and not APNs transport. APNs is accepting the push, but the device still does not surface the PushToTalk audio activation callback.
- The current strongest hypothesis is that this is at the Apple PushToTalk receiver activation boundary, not in our sender path.
- Important nuance:
  - warm/foreground-first runs have historically behaved better than truly fresh cold wake
  - the latest clean cold wake still stopped at `awaitingSystemActivation`

## Recommended next step

1. Stop changing backend/Worker code unless a new wake event stops showing `result = sent`.
2. Focus on the Avery-side PushToTalk activation boundary:
   - inspect `incomingPushResult(...)`
   - inspect `didActivate audioSession`
   - inspect anything that could still race or interfere with lock-screen activation
3. If no further app-owned simplification changes the outcome, treat this as an Apple/PTT limitation/bug and shift effort toward:
   - minimal repro quality diagnostics
   - product hardening around cold wake unreliability
   - documenting the warm-path workaround clearly
