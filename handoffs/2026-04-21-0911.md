  `https://beepbeep.to/v1/health`
  `https://mauscheff.unison-services.cloud/s/turbo/v1/health`

- Repo instructions are now stricter about mixed app/backend bugs:
  the docs explicitly require identifying the authoritative subsystem, inspecting both projections, adding invariants at the authoritative seam, and not accepting frontend-only masking for backend-owned truth.

  first wake succeeds and audio is heard; on release Blake shows `Preparing Audio`, appears to reconnect, then the second hold-to-talk fails with `target user has no connected receiving device on this channel`.
- In some runs Avery’s Apple PushToTalk UI was left disabled/busy on that later attempt.
- The new backend deployment targeted the stale one-sided connect/session bug, not this second-wake failure directly.
- No post-deploy physical-device retest has happened yet for:

  the stale one-sided connect/session issue had a real backend root cause and should now be less likely, but the second-wake failure may still involve app/PTT wake lifecycle, transport re-arm timing, or another backend wake/readiness transition that has not yet been isolated.
