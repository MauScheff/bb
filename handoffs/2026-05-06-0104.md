## Current truth

## What changed this session

- Diagnostics showed Direct QUIC was fast and packets were delivered, but pre-Apple mic samples were mostly silent/all-zero. The receiver also hit CoreAudio playback failure before Apple activation, while real sender Apple audio activation landed around 4.1 seconds in that trace.
- Apple-gated warm transmit now closes and deactivates the app-managed prewarm audio session before requesting `beginTransmitting`, while preserving the Direct QUIC transport path.
- Direct QUIC control-path checks are separated from "there is still a prewarmed media session", so stop/release signaling can still happen after the app-managed media session is intentionally closed.
- Apple-gated capture now records an explicit deferral until Apple audio activation instead of attempting early pre-activation capture.
- Receiver foreground Direct QUIC handling avoids reasserting Apple remote participant state in the app-managed fallback case, reducing audio-route contention.
- Playback startup got a small defensive improvement: system-activated playback-only sessions prime and reassert the playback node.
- Tests were added/updated around Apple-gated Direct QUIC deferral, abort-after-release, receiver Direct QUIC playback, and playback-node priming.

## What is not working

- First-hold startup is slow again, roughly five seconds on the latest device test.
- This is an accepted temporary regression because first-hold audio now works.
- Still unknown: the exact remaining source of the five-second delay in the final working build. The strongest hypothesis is still Apple PushToTalk/audio-session activation latency, but tomorrow's first step should verify this with fresh merged diagnostics instead of relying on the earlier no-audio trace.
