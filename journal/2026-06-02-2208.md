# Journal 2026-06-02 22:08

## Summary

Simulator fuzz seed `20260643` exposed a transmit-begin race where backend/channel refresh could clear the optimistic local press latch before the transmit reducer accepted `.pressRequested`. The fix makes that reducer handoff explicit so stale idle reconciliation does not erase a real held press during the handoff window.

## Problem

- Seed `20260643` failed at `a begins transmitting`.
- `@avery` stayed in `startingTransmit(stage: requestingLease)` while the backend lease was granted and then immediately cleaned up as "after release".
- Diagnostics showed `transmitPressActive` flipped false during `transmit-sync` before any scenario `endTransmit` action.

## Design formulation

- Owner: app transmit runtime/projection.
- Rule: an optimistic local press latch is valid while control-plane begin is being handed to the authoritative transmit reducer.
- Stale idle cleanup is still correct after that handoff completes; the bug was missing state for the asynchronous handoff interval.

## What changed

- Added `hasPendingControlPlaneBeginHandoff` to `TransmitRuntimeState`.
- `beginTransmit()` marks the handoff before launching the reducer task and clears it after `.pressRequested` is handled.
- `syncTransmitState()` no longer reconciles the press latch to idle while that handoff is pending.
- Added `syncTransmitStatePreservesPressLatchDuringControlPlaneBeginHandoff`.

## What worked

- The focused Swift test proves the latch survives the handoff window and is still cleared once the handoff marker is removed.
- The exact simulator replay for seed `20260643` now passes.
- The regression gate remains green.

## What not to repeat

- Do not weaken stale-idle reconciliation globally. That cleanup is useful; it only needed an explicit exception for a live reducer handoff.
- Do not treat a backend lease cleanup as the source when diagnostics show the local press state was lost before the grant path made its activation decision.
