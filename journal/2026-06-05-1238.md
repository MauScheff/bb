# Journal 2026-06-05 12:38

## Summary

Repeated foreground Fast Relay turns exposed a receive-side stale packet admission bug: packet media could wait in local app queues long after the user-level turn had become useless, then still reach playback and keep the receiver UI in a blocked state. The fix makes packet media freshness a first-class admission boundary while leaving admitted packet playout to the media engine.

## Problem

- Symptom: after several quick back-and-forth turns between `@mau` and `@bau`, one side could hear no audio while the receiver stayed as if the remote participant was still talking.
- Context: physical foreground-to-foreground Fast Relay stress, iPhone `@mau` and iPad `@bau`.
- Concrete diagnostics: `media.incoming_audio_queue_delay`, `incoming audio payload was delayed in the local receive queue`, `maxLocalQueueDelayMs=80206`, and backend transmit lease cleanup roughly 69 seconds late.
- Interpretation: the receiver did get media, but the app admitted it after a local stall. That is not jitter. It is stale evidence leaking across the user-level turn boundary.

## Design formulation

- A Talk Turn has phases: active, draining, closed.
- A packet is schedulable only while it is fresh for the current receive epoch.
- Once a packet exceeds the local admission deadline or sender-clock deadline, it becomes expired evidence. It may produce diagnostics, but it must not schedule playback, block the talk button, poison the next fresh packet, or stop the packet receive turn by itself.
- Admission freshness and playout freshness are separate boundaries:
  - App adapter: rejects stale packets before playback admission.
  - Media core / jitter buffer: handles fresh admitted packets, short gaps, reordering, PLC/FEC, and playout timing.
- Do not use the new NetEQ-style playout engine to compensate for 80-second app queue starvation. The playout engine should never receive that packet as audio.

## What changed

- Fast Relay QUIC datagram incoming audio now uses the same bounded pre-handler expiry as other relay audio paths.
- Direct QUIC incoming audio now has a bounded pre-handler expiry before app-level media handling.
- `PTTViewModel` now applies live backlog and sender-clock freshness checks to `.directQuic` and `.mediaRelayPacket` before playback admission.
- Packet media receives an app queue deadline, but not a duplicate media-session transport deadline after admission.
- Tests were updated from preserving stale packet media to rejecting it before playback, while preserving relay clients and active receive epochs.

## What worked

- Stale packet media is now dropped with `dropped-expired-live-backlog` or `dropped-expired-sender-clock-age`.
- Dropping expired packet media does not force-stop remote receive.
- A stale packet does not cancel a pending fresh playback completion.
- A fresh packet admitted to playback survives slow media-session work; stale-on-arrival packets still drop, and later fresh packets still play.

## What not to repeat

- Do not treat unordered packet media as timeless. Unordered does not mean unbounded.
- Do not pass the same outer stale-backlog deadline into packet playout after the packet has been admitted. That collapses app admission policy into media-core policy and can kill fresh packets during slow but valid playout work.
- Do not route physical missing-audio reports directly to jitter buffer work when diagnostics show app-level local queue delays measured in seconds. First decide whether the packet should have been admitted at all.
- Do not make stale packet drops clear the whole Fast Relay or Direct QUIC receive path. Expired packet media is a packet outcome, not necessarily a lane failure.
