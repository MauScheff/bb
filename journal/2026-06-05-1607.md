# Journal 2026-06-05 16:07

## Summary

Foreground fast-relay audio became reliable again after treating receiver playback readiness as the authoritative gate. The sender was healthy; the receiver could accept packets into an internal queue before Apple/PTT playback was actually ready, which made the first playback ACK too early and allowed live audio to age into the stale-backlog drop path.

## Problem

- Foreground fast-relay and Direct QUIC turns were mostly clear, but occasionally a full count to 10 produced no audible audio.
- Device logs showed the sender captured and delivered non-silent audio, and the receiver emitted a first playback ACK.
- The receiver later logged `Dropped incoming audio after transport delivery was cancelled` and `Incoming audio payload was delayed in the local receive queue`.
- The decisive timeline showed `PTT audio session activated` arriving several seconds after packets had already been accepted.

## Design formulation

- `connected` transport state is not equivalent to playback readiness.
- A first playback ACK must mean the receiver accepted audio into a path that can actually play, not merely into an internal pending queue.
- Foreground packet media should be governed by explicit receive epochs, freshness, and playback readiness. Swift task cancellation and pending decode queues are scheduling details, not user-visible turn state.
- If Apple/PTT playback is not ready in a foreground packet-media turn, use an explicit app-managed foreground receive fallback and flush buffered audio through that path.

## What changed

- Playback-only `ensureMediaSession` now reasserts receive startup even when media transport is already `connected`.
- Foreground packet media buffers while PTT playback is inactive, then starts an immediate app-managed foreground receive playback fallback for Apple-gated packet media.
- Immediate app-managed receive fallback logs as an intentional foreground playback path, not as an Apple activation timeout.
- Swift proofs now cover the foreground Direct QUIC receive fallback and the connected-but-playback-only startup assertion.

## What worked

- Physical retest between @mau and @bau became responsive and clear again after the receiver no longer ACKed from an unplayable pending queue.
- The device evidence matched the model: sender delivery was good, failure was receiver-side admission/readiness.
- The fix addresses the source boundary: playback readiness now gates ACK/playout, instead of compensating by widening stale-backlog deadlines.

## What not to repeat

- Do not interpret first ACK as proof of user-audible playback unless it comes after real playback admission.
- Do not make the live backlog threshold larger to hide readiness bugs. Very late audio is stale by product semantics.
- Do not treat `mediaConnectionState == .connected` as sufficient for a role-swapped receive path. It is a transport/session shell signal, not a playback-ready signal.
- Do not let packet-media receive semantics depend on raw `Task.isCancelled`; use epoch, lease, freshness, and playback readiness.
