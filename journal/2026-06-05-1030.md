# Journal 2026-06-05 10:30

## Summary

Fast Relay audio recovered when packet media stopped being expired by app-side freshness gates before playout. The durable rule is: packet lanes may report local queue delay, but playout owns packet lateness, jitter, gaps, and recovery after epoch and lease validation.

## Problem

- Foreground Fast Relay calls between `@mau` iPhone and `@bau` iPad degraded after a few rapid talk turns.
- Typical symptom: one direction was clean, the immediate response played only a few delayed numbers or no audio.
- The receiver sometimes remained visually stuck as if the remote participant was still talking.
- Concrete diagnostics included:
  - `media.incoming_audio_queue_delay`
  - `media.playout_repeated_underrun`
  - `media.incoming_audio_chunk_gap`
  - `PTT: active remote participant clear failed`

## Design formulation

- Owner: packet-lane admission and playout boundary.
- Packet media is not ordered relay media. It must not be made reliable by deleting "late" packets at the view-model ingress.
- After channel, device, receive epoch, and backend transmit lease validation, packet payloads should be admitted to the media session/playout engine.
- Local queue delay is diagnostic evidence for overload. It is not an admissibility rule for Direct QUIC or Fast Relay packet media.
- Ordered fallback lanes may keep stricter freshness expiry because old queued ordered audio can create audible stale playback.

## What changed

- Disabled view-model live-backlog and sender-clock expiry for `.directQuic` and `.mediaRelayPacket`.
- Removed the Fast Relay datagram handler queue expiration before the app handler.
- Preserved delayed Direct QUIC Opus payloads and recorded `preserved-expired-live-backlog` instead of dropping them.
- Kept defensive Direct QUIC backlog reset behavior for non-Opus payloads.
- Prevented expired packet-media handling from stopping the remote receive turn or tearing down the relay client.
- Updated packet-lane tests from "drop late packet before playback" to "admit packet to playout; record lateness if useful."

## What worked

- Device logs showed the sender captured and dispatched real audio while the receiver initially accepted packets quickly, then started dropping many packets before playout as local queue delay grew.
- Removing packet-lane pre-playout expiry matched the evidence: after the patch, rapid foreground Fast Relay turns between `@mau` and `@bau` were clean again.
- Focused tests now prove that stale-looking Fast Relay packets do not poison the receive turn, cancel pending playback, stop receive state, or close the relay client.

## What not to repeat

- Do not tune packet-lane expiry thresholds as the primary fix for lossy Fast Relay audio.
- Do not treat `media.incoming_audio_queue_delay` on a packet lane as a reason to delete Opus media before playout.
- Do not let packet-lane backlog diagnostics mutate receive ownership, active participant state, or transport path state.
- Do not combine encryption, lane upgrade/downgrade changes, raw datagram transport, and foreground/background testing in one unisolated change.
- Do not use physical devices as the only proof once the failure can be represented by packet-admission tests.
