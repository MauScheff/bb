# Journal 2026-06-06 00:32

## Summary

Physical Fast Relay testing kept producing late receive queue and stuck-listening symptoms after several rapid turns. The durable lesson is that each talk turn must be isolated by explicit receive/playback epochs and bounded non-main hot paths; stale ordered fallback audio must never be accepted as a new turn merely because its sequence number restarted.

The overnight simulator fuzz then found a separate selected-conversation convergence bug: an accepted backend join could briefly coexist with stale pending Beep projection evidence and local Apple Device PTT evidence. The engine replay was clean; the app projection was wrong to let stale Beep residue tear down a fresh accepted join.

## Problem

- Physical runs between `@mau` and `@bau` repeatedly showed clean first turns followed by no-audio or delayed-tail turns after rapid back-and-forth.
- The concrete invariant language was:
  - `media.incoming_audio_queue_delay`
  - `Incoming audio payload was delayed in the local receive queue`
  - `dropped-expired-live-backlog`
  - `dropped-expired-sender-clock-age`
  - ignored playback ACK without a pending expectation
  - receiver UI temporarily stuck as if the peer was still talking.
- The failure was most visible on Fast Relay, but the design problem was not Fast Relay-specific: live audio, ACKs, diagnostics, transport fallback, and UI projection were still capable of piling up around shared app state.
- The overnight simulator failure appeared at seed `20261068` under `/tmp/turbo-scenario-fuzz/20260606-042917-9cb86771/seed-20261068`.
- The seed failed in the app/scenario lane while engine trace extraction and replay were clean, which classified the owner as app projection/effect convergence rather than TurboEngine reducer logic.

## Design formulation

- Owner: app media/runtime state for receive admission and playback gating; lower transport lanes only deliver candidate payloads.
- Contract: a fresh talk turn is fresh because an explicit turn/receive boundary resets the gate, not because a transport emits low sequence numbers.
- Contract: ordered reliable fallback is not allowed to restart contact-wide playback after packet media unless the receiver has crossed an explicit boundary.
- Contract: stale receive backlog incidents are bounded evidence, not new authority. They must not poison the next fresh turn.
- Contract: the hot path should be modeled and fuzzed across all lanes. The user-visible property is lane-independent: after one turn drains, the next turn should sound as good as the first unless the new turn itself has loss.
- Owner: selected-conversation projection owns the user-visible phase while backend join, local Device PTT, pending Beep, and backend membership evidence converge.
- Contract: once a pending Beep has been accepted and the local Apple session is aligned with backend self-membership or backend join-settling evidence, that Beep is stale acceptance residue. It must not dominate the live projection or schedule Device PTT teardown.

- `audio-packet-fuzz` found a real stale fallback-tail bug: relay websocket sequence `1` was accepted after packet media sequence `41`.
- The fix moved the rule to the right semantic place. Packet/order freshness is not a transport-specific heuristic; it is contact-wide playback authority guarded by explicit turn boundaries.
- The full audio packet/playout fuzz gate now passes after the fix.
- The seed `20261068` failure was promoted into focused Swift tests, fixed, and replayed green.
- The resumed simulator fuzz run passed all `37/37` follow-up seeds from `20261069` through `20261105` under `/tmp/turbo-scenario-fuzz/20260606-054752-cef759af`.
- Passing seeds covered delayed receiver-ready, dropped transmit-start, unexpected system transmit end, missing PTT deactivation callback, explicit disconnect, background/foreground transitions, websocket slow-hedge, stale channel refresh after leave, and target-device-disconnected paths.
- Simulator Direct QUIC/media encryption identity provisioning still reports keychain entitlement `-34018`; that is simulator-only noise. This fuzz proved app/backend/engine convergence over relay/websocket paths, not physical Apple audio I/O or production Direct QUIC identity.

## What not to repeat

- Do not treat a sequence restart on an ordered fallback lane as proof of a new user turn.
- Do not debug the physical phones in a loop without promoting the observed invariant into corpus replay or fuzz mutation.
- Do not make Fast Relay the only non-blocking lane. The contract is lane-independent: Direct QUIC, Fast Relay, and ordered fallback all feed a bounded admission/playback model.
- Do not call the iPad home-screen return a normal app crash unless there is a fresh `.ips` crash. The observed shape matched Apple PTT/lifecycle restoration after receive ordering was violated.
- Do not let pending Beep relationship evidence remain authoritative after an accepted local/backend join has begun converging. At that point it is acceptance residue, not user intent.
