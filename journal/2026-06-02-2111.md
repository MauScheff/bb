# Journal 2026-06-02 21:11

## Summary

Simulator fuzz seed `20260610` exposed an over-strict wake-capable receiver invariant: the app and offline diagnostics treated an unresolved outgoing Beep as a stuck non-connectable UI even though backend readiness had legitimately raced ahead and the flow later converged.

## Problem

- `just reliability-fuzz-local-overnight 20260605 500` failed at simulator seed `20260610`.
- Scenario execution passed, engine trace extraction passed, and engine replay passed.
- Strict diagnostics failed on `selected.wake_capable_receiver_ui_not_connectable` while `selectedConversationPhase=outgoingBeep`, `selectedConversationRelationship=outgoingBeep(requestCount: 1)`, `backendChannelStatus=waiting-for-peer`, and `remoteWakeCapabilityKind=wake-capable`.

## Design formulation

- Ownership: app diagnostics and offline replay classification, not engine or backend truth.
- State-machine rule: a wake-capable backend channel is only a stuck selected-conversation UI when the selected phase is truly stale: `idle`, or `outgoingBeep` after the outgoing Beep relationship has resolved.
- In-flight outgoing Beep, pending actions, backend join settling, and local Device PTT evidence are ordering windows, not invariant failures.

## What changed

- Narrowed `selected.wake_capable_receiver_ui_not_connectable` in app diagnostics.
- Mirrored the same rule in offline merged diagnostics.
- Added Swift and Python regression tests for outstanding outgoing Beep with wake-capable receiver evidence.

## What worked

- The exact failed artifact replayed cleanly after the detector was narrowed.
- Engine replay stayed green, confirming the issue was diagnostic classification rather than the Rust/Unison kernel or engine reducer.

## What not to repeat

- Do not classify every `outgoingBeep` as disconnected UI. It still carries unresolved relationship evidence.
- Do not fix this by forcing the selected projection to skip the outgoing Beep state; that would hide a real user-visible request state and make ordering harder to reason about.
