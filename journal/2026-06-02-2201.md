# Journal 2026-06-02 22:01

## Summary

Simulator fuzz seed `20260642` exposed that stale backend joined refreshes could reopen the engine conversation after an explicit disconnect once the pending leave had been locally cleared. The durable rule is that the recent system-leave tombstone must also block backend joined refreshes, not only automatic local PTT restores.

## Problem

- `just reliability-fuzz-local-overnight 20260631 500` failed on seed `20260642`.
- The failing step was `disconnect clears joined state`.
- Last mismatch showed both participants back at `selectedConversationPhase=ready`, `selectedConversationStatus=Connected`, `isJoined=true`, `backendSelfJoined=true`, `backendPeerJoined=true`, and active system sessions.
- Strict diagnostics and engine trace replay both passed; the failure was an app projection/control-plane race, not an engine invariant failure.

## Design Formulation

- App-owned local leave intent creates a short tombstone after system leave.
- Backend joined refreshes are not authoritative enough to reopen engine joined state while that recent-leave tombstone is active.
- This is a better rule than stretching `pendingAction.leave`: local pending leave may clear before all delayed or stale backend refreshes have drained.

## What Changed

- `syncEngineJoinedConversation` now ignores backend joined refreshes when `hasStaleSystemRejoinSuppression` is active for the contact/channel.
- Added a focused Swift regression for stale backend joined refresh after recent system leave.

## What Worked

- The exact replay artifact passed after the fix.
- The focused regression proves the behavior below the simulator lane.
- The reliability regression gate passed, including engine tests, engine fuzz corpus, trace replay, invariant registry checks, Python reliability utilities, and targeted Swift tests.

## What Not To Repeat

- Do not treat a locally cleared pending leave as proof that all stale backend membership evidence has drained.
- Do not fix this by weakening the scenario expectation; the user-facing state must not resurrect an explicit disconnect.
