# Journal 2026-06-03 16:26

## Summary

Foreground packet media became live again when Direct QUIC and Fast Relay packet lanes were treated as unordered, best-effort media with a single immediate playback contract. The fix was not to tune retries or add more lifecycle patches; it was to make lane class determine buffering, playback scheduling, continuity clocks, stale-drop behavior, and diagnostics pressure.

## Problem

- Same-room foreground calls selected Direct QUIC, but one direction dropped words or arrived seconds late.
- Device diagnostics showed packets were received, then delayed by app/Apple activation machinery:
  - `Buffered foreground receive audio chunk until PTT activation`
  - `media.incoming_audio_chunk_gap`
  - `transmit.first_audio_ack_without_expectation`
  - `transmit.first_audio_playback_ack_missing`
  - repeated standby duplicate diagnostics
- The visible symptom looked like network loss, but the decisive contradiction was local: foreground packet audio had already arrived and was still being held behind unrelated receive/activation and playback waits.

## Design formulation

- Transport class owns receive policy.
- `direct-quic` and `media-relay-packet` are unordered packet media:
  - play immediately in foreground when a media session exists or can be prepared;
  - never wait for Apple/PTT activation unless an active fallback startup explicitly requires it;
  - never await playback completion on the packet handler path;
  - measure continuity from ingress receive time, not delayed admission or playback completion time;
  - an expired packet drop must not stop the whole receive turn or clear the live receive epoch.
- `relay-websocket` and `media-relay-tcp` remain ordered lanes:
  - they may preserve startup audio, respect system activation, and use ordered backlog policy;
  - WebSocket fallback may keep slow sequential frames playable because it is the conservative reliability lane.

This is the right shape because the core rule is algebraic: lane type determines lawful behavior. Packet lanes optimize for liveness and freshness; ordered lanes optimize for completeness and sequencing. The app should not rediscover that policy at every lifecycle branch.

## What changed

- Added `IncomingAudioPayloadTransport.isUnreliablePacketMedia`.
- Passed incoming transport into foreground Apple/PTT activation buffering decisions.
- Bypassed foreground Apple/PTT activation buffering for Direct QUIC and Fast Relay packet media when app-managed playback is the correct foreground path.
- Scheduled packet playback completion asynchronously so packet admission does not wait for slow playback.
- Preserved `ingressReceivedAtNanoseconds` in accepted ingress packets and used it for engine receive ticks and continuity checks.
- Budgeted duplicate standby plaintext diagnostics so diagnostics do not become audio-path load.
- Preserved packet receive epoch after expired packet drops; ordered lanes still use the existing stop/drain behavior.
- Updated Apple-gated tests so ordered WebSocket cases prove buffering, while packet tests prove immediate live playback.

## What worked

- Physical retest with `@mau` and `@bau` over Direct QUIC became fast, responsive, and clear in both directions.
- The automated proofs now encode the design split:
  - packet lanes stay live under slow playback and stale packet pressure;
  - ordered fallback lanes keep their buffering semantics;
  - ingress stale/drop contracts remain explicit.
- The fix addressed the source because it removed invalid waits from the packet-media contract instead of adding a timeout, retry, or diagnostic suppression around late audio.

## What not to repeat

- Do not treat all receive lanes as if Apple/PTT activation is the universal gate. That makes packet media inherit ordered/wake semantics and creates avoidable latency.
- Do not use app admission or playback completion time as the continuity clock for packet media. That fabricates network gaps when the app is merely busy.
- Do not stop a live packet receive turn because one packet expired. Dropping stale packets is normal for unordered media; ending the turn is a control-plane decision.
- Do not let high-volume diagnostics run unbounded on the audio hot path. Diagnostics must preserve evidence without becoming the failure.
