# Journal 2026-06-05 12:57

## Summary

Fast Relay receive admission now treats each talk turn as a fresh receive epoch: prior-turn audio is disposable at the epoch boundary, while current-turn startup audio gets a real Apple/PTT activation budget before it is considered stale.

## Problem

- Physical-device Fast Relay tests between `@mau` and `@bau` degraded after repeated turns, and sometimes a first current-turn packet was dropped before playback.
- Receiver diagnostics showed `media.incoming_audio_queue_delay`, often as `Incoming audio payload was delayed in the local receive queue`.
- The broken model conflated moderate current-turn startup delay with stale prior-turn backlog. A 650ms packet queue threshold could expire valid audio while Apple PTT activation or playback recovery was still starting.

## Design formulation

- Epoch freshness is the hard semantic boundary. A new receive epoch must not depend on queued work from a previous turn.
- Time freshness is an admission budget, not the turn identity. A current receive epoch can tolerate startup delay while Apple/PTT and playback settle.
- For packet media, stale packets should fail closed before playback; admitted packet media should not be retroactively invalidated after playback acceptance just because the async path crossed the same deadline.

## What changed

- Raised Fast Relay incoming handler expiration from 650ms to 2s.
- Made `.mediaRelayPacket` use the normal incoming live-audio backlog budget instead of the 650ms packet diagnostic threshold.
- Kept stale packet drops and diagnostics for packets older than the receive admission budget.
- Preserved accepted packet media after playback admission, while still dropping stale packet arrivals.
- Updated tests so moderate delay is current-turn startup and old delay is genuinely stale.

## What worked

- `mediaRelayPacketCurrentEpochStartupDelayUsesReceiveStartupBudget` proves an 800ms current Fast Relay packet is accepted during receive startup.
- `defaultMediaRelayPacketQueueBacklogDropsObservedStalePayloadBeforePlayback` now proves a 2.2s Fast Relay packet is dropped with `media.incoming_audio_queue_delay`.
- Relay queue tests prove expired pending datagram and TCP-ordered handlers are skipped before app playback.
- Receive-turn preservation tests prove stale packet drops do not stop the active receive epoch or cancel fresh packets behind them.

## What not to repeat

- Do not use a short diagnostic threshold as the fatal current-turn startup budget.
- Do not remove packet freshness checks entirely; stale prior-turn audio must still be rejected.
- Do not interpret a moderate local queue delay as old-turn evidence unless the packet is outside the epoch or receive admission budget.
