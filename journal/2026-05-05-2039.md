# Journal 2026-05-05 20:39

## Summary

Warm Direct QUIC transmit was fast enough to send packets immediately, but in Apple-gated PushToTalk mode those packets were mostly silence until Apple activated the audio session. The fix was to preserve the warm Direct QUIC path while deferring app-managed capture and the transmit-start signal until Apple audio activation.

## Problem

- Physical devices connected and both sides quickly showed talking, but the receiver heard no audio.
- Merged diagnostics showed the receiver was not dropping audio: it received, decoded, and scheduled buffers.
- Sender diagnostics showed the real failure:
  - first two captured/enqueued payloads were tiny but non-silent,
  - subsequent payloads were repeated all-zero PCM,
  - Apple `didActivateAudioSession` arrived very late, nearly at release.
- The misleading state was: Direct QUIC transport was live, but Apple had not yet granted usable microphone ownership.

## Design formulation

- Apple-gated transmit has an important invariant: receiver-visible "talking" must mean Apple has activated the PushToTalk audio session and the sender is capturing real app audio.
- Direct QUIC transport readiness is not the same fact as audio-capture readiness.
- In Apple-gated mode, pre-activation app capture is not authoritative. It can be fast, but diagnostics proved it can emit silence while making the UI and receiver believe speech is live.
- The sender owns this invariant because it decides when to begin capture and when to emit the Direct QUIC lease-bypassed `transmit-start` signal.

## What changed

- `startAppleBeganWarmDirectQuicCaptureIfPossible` now checks `isPTTAudioSessionActive`.
- If Apple audio is not active, it records `early-audio-capture-deferred-until-system-activation` and returns without starting capture.
- The regression test now asserts:
  - no `startSendingAudio` before Apple activation,
  - no `transmit-start-signal-sent` before Apple activation,
  - normal `audio-capture-start-completed` after Apple activation.
- PCM diagnostics added earlier remain useful for distinguishing silent sender capture from receiver playback/network failure.

## What worked

- Merged diagnostics across both devices were decisive: the receiver scheduled the same payloads the sender emitted, and those payloads became silent at the source.
- Fixing the source state boundary worked on physical devices: after the change, warm hold-to-talk produced audible audio again.
- Keeping Direct QUIC warm while deferring Apple-gated capture preserved the fast transport path without falsely advertising silent audio.

## What not to repeat

- Do not treat "Direct QUIC can send bytes" as equivalent to "the user is talking" in Apple-gated mode.
- Do not send receiver-visible transmit-start merely because `didBeginTransmitting` fired if Apple audio is not active yet.
- Do not patch this as a receiver playback problem when diagnostics show payloads are already silent before they leave the sender.
