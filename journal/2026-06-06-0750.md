# Journal 2026-06-06 07:50

## Summary

Simulator fuzz exposed two app-state convergence bugs behind the physical-device "no audio / stuck listening" reports: valid transmit activation could be rejected by a stale runtime press latch, and stale Beep/PTT projection evidence could tear down an already backend-ready call. The fix makes reducer/backend-ready truth dominate fragile local latches and stale relationship projections.

## Problem

- Physical-device testing showed no-audio turns, stuck listening state, disabled talk buttons, delayed recovery, and repeated `media contract liveness failed` reports after fast back-and-forth turns.
- Simulator fuzz reproduced adjacent deterministic failures where a scenario timed out even though backend/control evidence eventually showed the call should converge.
- Seed `20261118` showed a backend transmit lease being treated as arriving after release because `transmitRuntime.isPressingTalk` had been cleared while the reducer still held the current press.
- Seed `20261189` showed a stale incoming Beep relationship projection triggering `Tearing down invalid Device PTT session` even though backend readiness proved both members were joined, the peer device was connected, and transmit was allowed.

## Design formulation

- Transmit activation authority belongs to the reducer-owned transmit coordinator plus explicit stop semantics, not to a best-effort runtime latch alone.
- A backend-ready established conversation is stronger evidence than stale local Beep/PTT relationship projections.
- Stale relationship evidence may still dominate a stale self-only local session, but it must not tear down a session once backend truth proves `local membership + peer membership + peer device + can transmit`.
- This keeps turn/call state algebraic: old projections become no-ops against newer established truth instead of destructive commands.

## What changed

- `PTTViewModel.shouldActivateBackendTransmitLease` now accepts activation when either the runtime latch, reducer press state, or matching system handoff proves the current press is still live.
- `ConversationDerivationContext` now exposes `backendShowsEstablishedReadyConversation`.
- Pending Beep projection and reconciled teardown now defer to backend-established readiness instead of allowing stale local evidence to dominate.
- Added focused regressions for lost runtime press latch activation and stale incoming Beep against backend-ready state.

## What worked

- Failing seeds replayed green after the fixes.
- A 50-seed simulator fuzz continuation from the previous failure point passed with reorder, duplicate, slow-control, leave/join, wake, and transport-lane interleavings.
- The narrow tests prove both directions of the Beep rule: stale incoming Beep still dominates a stale self-only local session, but not an established backend-ready call.

## What not to repeat

- Do not treat UI/local relationship evidence as equal to backend-ready membership once the backend has proven an established conversation.
- Do not rely on auxiliary runtime latches as the sole source of truth for current transmit intent.
- Do not interpret physical "no audio" reports only as media/playout bugs; state convergence failures can make audio impossible before the media engine has a chance to be correct.
