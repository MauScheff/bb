# Journal 2026-05-05 13:59

## Summary

Foreground-to-foreground Direct QUIC transmit worked again after narrowing the fix back to the clean ownership model: Apple owns the PushToTalk audio/session handoff, Direct QUIC owns fast early transport, the media session owns capture health, and the view model must not force audio-route mutation during Apple's pending handoff window.

## Problem

- Foreground-to-foreground Direct QUIC became fast but broken: sender entered `Waiting for microphone...`, receiver showed talking/receiving behavior, and little or no audio was heard.
- Merged diagnostics showed the sender started fast and captured initial audio, then the new reassertion path logged `Media session audio route refresh failed error=unable to create capture converter`.
- That error happened after `Reasserting prewarmed Direct QUIC capture during PTT handoff`, proving the attempted fix had introduced a second route mutation during Apple's ownership window.

## Design formulation

- A transmit attempt has one lifecycle and one explicit release.
- Apple owns the PushToTalk audio/session transition.
- The Direct QUIC bridge may start early because it is transport work, not Apple session ownership.
- `PTTViewModel` may express intent: "this transmit attempt should still be sending."
- `PCMWebSocketMediaSession` owns whether capture is healthy and whether media setup/restart is safe.
- Backend transmit projection is shared truth, but a local explicit release must suppress stale backend `self-transmitting` until backend convergence.

The important distinction: "continue sending on the current capture path" can be idempotent; "rebuild the audio route while Apple is activating PTT" is not.

## What changed

- Kept the prewarmed Direct QUIC bridge before Apple PTT activation so first-audio latency stays low.
- Kept immediate peer `transmitStop` signaling on release, before waiting for slow backend `endTransmit`.
- Kept the guard that prevents stale backend `self-transmitting` from overriding an explicit local stop.
- Removed the pending-handoff call to `audioRouteDidChange()`, because it force-rebuilt AVAudio capture during Apple's handoff.
- Updated the regression so pending-handoff reassertion must not call route refresh.

## What worked

- Physical foreground-to-foreground Direct QUIC worked after removing the forced route refresh.
- The successful shape preserves speed without taking Apple out of the loop.
- The fix is structural: it restores ownership boundaries instead of adding another retry around AVAudio setup failure.

## What not to repeat

- Do not bypass Apple/native PushToTalk for foreground Direct QUIC just because the transport is warm.
- Do not call `audioRouteDidChange()` as a generic "try harder" operation during pending system transmit activation.
- Do not let stale backend `self-transmitting` resurrect local UI state after the user has released.
