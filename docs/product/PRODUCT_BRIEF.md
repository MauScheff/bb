## Overview

Turbo makes real conversations easy to start without calling. Users signal intent to talk; when both are ready, they enter a simple structured voice interaction.

## Problem

Modern communication tools force a tradeoff:

| Tool | Strength | Weakness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Messaging (for example WhatsApp) | Flexible | Slow, uncertain, fragmented |
| Calls | Immediate | Intrusive, high pressure |
| Voice notes | Convenient | Delayed, no real interaction |

Result: conversations are delayed, avoided, or inefficient.

## Solution

Interaction model:

- Send a Beep without interrupting.
- The recipient responds when ready.
- Both join a live, structured conversation.

Benefits:

- No pressure
- No back-and-forth lag
- No talking over each other

## Core Value Proposition

**Easy to start. Easy to talk.**

Meaning:

- Conversations happen without friction.
- There is no need to commit to a call upfront.
- There is no need to manage threads or messages.
- You just talk when it works.

## Key Benefits

- **Clarity:** one person speaks at a time
- **Low effort:** minimal interaction required
- **Certainty:** conversations actually happen
- **Flexibility:** join when ready
- **Usability:** works in real-life situations like walking or multitasking

## Why Now

- Calls are increasingly avoided due to social pressure.
- Messaging dominates but creates delays and overload.
- Voice usage is rising, but current tools are inefficient.
- Modern platforms now enable seamless, reliable voice experiences.

People want to talk, but not in the way current tools force them to.

### Behavior

### 2. Behavior-Driven Entry

## Product Principles

- **Simple:** no setup, no complexity
- **Non-intrusive:** respects attention

- **Predictable:** always clear what is happening

**Feels like infrastructure, not an app.**

## Product Description

### One Sentence

A simple way to start real conversations without calling.

### Short Description

Signal you want to talk, and connect when both are ready in a clear, structured voice conversation.

### Detailed Description

Today’s communication tools force a choice between messaging, which is flexible but slow, and calling, which is immediate but intrusive.

Turbo introduces a new layer: users signal intent to talk, and when both participants are ready, they enter a structured voice conversation that is easy to follow and fits naturally into real life.

The result is a simpler, more reliable way to communicate, one that reduces friction and makes conversations easier to start and complete.

## Vision

To become the default way people initiate conversations, sitting between messaging and calling as a standard layer of communication.

## Key Insight

People do not avoid talking. They avoid the friction of starting a conversation.
